About UPSC

UPSC stands for Union Public Service Commission. It is India's top government recruitment organization that conducts prestigious examinations for civil services, defense services and other central government departments.

UPSC exams are considered among the toughest and most respected exams in India.

Top UPSC Posts Explained

IAS Officer

IAS officers handle administration, district management, policy implementation and government operations.

IPS Officer

IPS officers manage police departments, law and order, crime investigation and public safety.

IFS Officer

IFS officers represent India in foreign countries and handle diplomatic relations.

IRS Officer

IRS officers work in income tax and customs departments.

UPSC Eligibility Criteria

Educational Qualification

  • Graduation required for Civil Services, CDS, CAPF and EPFO
  • 12th Pass required for NDA
  • Engineering Degree required for IES
  • MBBS required for CMS

Age Limit

  • 21 to 32 years for Civil Services
  • Age relaxation available for reserved categories

UPSC Exam Stages

1. Preliminary Exam

Objective type exam with General Studies and CSAT papers.

2. Mains Exam

Descriptive written examination including essays and optional subjects.

3. Interview

Personality test conducted by UPSC board members.

UPSC Preparation Tips

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Which is the best UPSC job?

IAS is considered the most prestigious UPSC post in India.

Can 12th pass students apply for UPSC?

Yes, students can apply for NDA after 12th.

Is UPSC the toughest exam in India?

Yes, UPSC Civil Services Examination is considered one of the toughest exams in India.

How many attempts are allowed in UPSC?

General category candidates get 6 attempts in Civil Services Examination.
